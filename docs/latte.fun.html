latte.fun

A function with domain T and codomain U has type (==> T U).

This namespace provides some important properties about such functions.

bijective

(bijective [T :type] [U :type] [f (==> T U)])
(and (injective T U f) (surjective T U f))

Definition: A bijective function.

bijective-is-injective

(bijective-is-injective [T :type] [U :type] [f (==> T U)])
(==> (bijective T U f) (injective T U f))

Theorem: A bijection is an injection.

bijective-is-surjective

(bijective-is-surjective [T :type] [U :type] [f (==> T U)])
(==> (bijective T U f) (surjective T U f))

Theorem: A bijection is a surjection.

bijective-unique

(bijective-unique [T :type] [U :type] [f (==> T U)])
(==>
 (bijective T U f)
 (forall [y U] (q/unique T (lambda [x T] (equal U (f x) y)))))

Theorem: A bijective function has exactly one antecedent for each image.

compose

(compose [T :type] [U :type] [V :type] [f (==> U V)] [g [==> T U]])
(lambda [x T] (f (g x)))

Definition: The composition of two functions.

compose-injective

(compose-injective [T :type] [U :type] [V :type] [f (==> U V)] [g [==> T U]])
(==>
 (injective U V f)
 (injective T U g)
 (injective T V (compose T U V f g)))

Theorem: The composition of two injective functions is injective.

injective

(injective [T :type] [U :type] [f (==> T U)])
(forall [x y T] (==> (equal U (f x) (f y)) (equal T x y)))

Definition: An injective function.

injective-single

(injective-single [T :type] [U :type] [f (==> T U)])
(==>
 (injective T U f)
 (forall [y U] (q/single T (lambda [x T] (equal U (f x) y)))))

Theorem: An injective function has at most one antecedent for each image.

inverse

(inverse [T :type] [U :type] [f (==> T U)] [b (bijective T U f)])
(lambda
 [y U]
 (q/the
  T
  (lambda [x T] (equal U (f x) y))
  ((bijective-unique T U f) b y)))

Definition: The inverse of function f.

inverse-bijective

(inverse-bijective [T :type] [U :type] [f (==> T U)] [b (bijective T U f)])
(bijective U T (inverse T U f b))

Theorem: The inverse of a bijection is a bijection.

inverse-injective

(inverse-injective [T :type] [U :type] [f (==> T U)] [b (bijective T U f)])
(injective U T (inverse T U f b))

Theorem: The inverse function of a bijection, is injective.

inverse-prop

(inverse-prop [T :type] [U :type] [f (==> T U)] [b (bijective T U f)])
(forall [y U] (equal U (f ((inverse T U f b) y)) y))

Theorem: The basic property of the inverse function.

inverse-prop-conv

(inverse-prop-conv [T :type] [U :type] [f (==> T U)] [b (bijective T U f)])
(forall [x T] (equal T ((inverse T U f b) (f x)) x))

Theorem: The basic property of the inverse function, the converse of inverse-prop.

inverse-surjective

(inverse-surjective [T :type] [U :type] [f (==> T U)] [b (bijective T U f)])
(surjective U T (inverse T U f b))

Theorem: The inverse function of a bijection, is surjective.

surjective

(surjective [T :type] [U :type] [f (==> T U)])
(forall [y U] (exists [x T] (equal U (f x) y)))

Definition: A surjective function.
