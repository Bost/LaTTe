latte.rel

A relation between elements of a given type T, is formalized with type (==> T T :type).

The type (==> T U :type) for arbitrary types T and U gives the relations between elements of T and elements of U.

With an extra property, this is also the type of functional relations, given by the type (==> T U).

This namespace provides some important properties about such relations.

bijective

(bijective [T :type] [U :type] [F (==> T U)])
(and (injective T U F) (surjective T U F))

Definition

A bijective function.

bijective-is-injective

(bijective-is-injective [T :type] [U :type] [F (==> T U)])
(==> (bijective T U F) (injective T U F))

Theorem

A bijection is an injection.

bijective-is-surjective

(bijective-is-surjective [T :type] [U :type] [F (==> T U)])
(==> (bijective T U F) (surjective T U F))

Theorem

A bijection is a surjection.

compose

(compose [T :type] [U :type] [V :type] [f (==> U V)] [g [==> T U]])

The composition of two functions.

compose-injective

(compose-injective [T :type] [U :type] [V :type] [f (==> U V)] [g [==> T U]])
(==>
 (injective U V f)
 (injective T U g)
 (injective T V (compose T U V f g)))

Theorem

The composition of two injective functions is injective.
